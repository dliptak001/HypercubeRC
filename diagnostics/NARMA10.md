# NARMA-10 Nonlinear Benchmark

## What this diagnostic measures

NARMA-10 (Nonlinear AutoRegressive Moving Average, order 10) is the
standard reservoir computing benchmark for combined memory and nonlinear
computation. The target sequence is generated by the recurrence:

    y(t+1) = 0.3*y(t) + 0.05*y(t)*sum(y(t-i), i=0..9) + 1.5*u(t-9)*u(t) + 0.1

where u(t) is a random input in [0, 0.5].

**The task:** given the input u(t), predict the target y(t) from reservoir
states. The readout must reconstruct a signal that depends on 10 steps of
history and nonlinear products of past values.

**The metric:** NRMSE. Standard ESN results range from 0.2 to 0.4.

## Why this benchmark matters

NARMA-10 is harder than Mackey-Glass because it demands three capabilities
simultaneously:

1. **Memory depth.** The u(t-9)*u(t) term requires remembering input from
   10 steps ago. A reservoir with short fading memory will fail here.

2. **Nonlinear computation.** The y(t)*sum(...) and u(t-9)*u(t) terms are
   products — a purely linear system cannot compute them. The reservoir's
   tanh nonlinearity must encode these interactions in its state vector.

3. **Temporal integration.** The autoregressive y(t) feedback means the
   target depends on its own history, not just the input history.

A reservoir that can only remember (high MC) OR only compute nonlinearly
will fail — it must do both.

## How it works

1. Generate NARMA-10 input/target sequences (deterministic per seed).
2. Scale inputs to [-1, +1] for reservoir injection (raw NARMA inputs
   are in [0, 0.5]).
3. Run the reservoir and collect raw states (N = 2^DIM floats per step).
4. Train the HCNN readout on 70%, test on 30%.
5. Report NRMSE.

Uses a single per-DIM seed selected by 500-seed survey (`SurveyedSeed<DIM>()`).

## Sample results

Run with HCNN readout (`HRCCNNBaseline<DIM>()` config):

| DIM | N    | HCNN NRMSE |
|-----|------|------------|
| 7   | 128  | 0.218      |
| 8   | 256  | 0.152      |
| 9   | 512  | 0.117      |
| 10  | 1024 | 0.087      |

## What to look for

- **NRMSE improves with DIM.** More neurons = deeper memory + richer
  dynamics. The HCNN readout discovers nonlinear features via learned
  convolution, breaking through the plateau that linear readouts hit.

- **HCNN learns the quadratic interactions** that the NARMA-10 recurrence
  demands (product terms u(t-9)*u(t) and y(t)*Σy), without hand-crafted
  feature engineering.

## HCNN seed-lottery variance shrinks with DIM

The default per-DIM seeds wired into `NARMA10::DefaultSeed()` come from a
HRCCNN survey (`CnnSeedSurvey`, HRCCNNBaseline config, 2000 epochs) over
the rank-1 reservoir from the 500-seed survey: 50 CNN seeds at DIM 5-8,
20 CNN seeds at DIM 9+ (where the seed lottery is effectively cosmetic).
The dispersion collapses monotonically as the reservoir grows:

| DIM | N    | mean   | std     | range   | CV     | best seed |
|-----|------|--------|---------|---------|--------|-----------|
| 5   | 32   | 0.4075 | 0.02319 | 0.08953 | 5.69%  | 21        |
| 6   | 64   | 0.3511 | 0.01849 | 0.08448 | 5.27%  | 34        |
| 7   | 128  | 0.2329 | 0.00768 | 0.03091 | 3.30%  | 6         |
| 8   | 256  | 0.1603 | 0.00414 | 0.01738 | 2.58%  | 2         |
| 9   | 512  | 0.1368 | 0.00209 | 0.00689 | 1.53%  | 20        |
| 10  | 1024 | 0.1239 | 0.00132 | 0.00440 | 1.06%  | 2         |

Trends:

- **Std roughly halves per DIM step at the larger sizes** (0.0185 → 0.0077
  → 0.0041 → 0.0021 → 0.0013). The steepest drop is 6 → 7 (−58%); the
  halving rule holds cleanly from DIM 7 onward, attenuating slightly at
  9 → 10 (−37%, not quite a halving).
- **Range tracks std**: nearly flat from DIM 5 → 6, then ~3× tighter at
  DIM 7, ~5× tighter at DIM 8, ~13× tighter at DIM 9, ~20× tighter at DIM 10.
- **CV (std / mean) also shrinks** from 5.7% to 1.1%, so the tightening is
  not just a mean-scaling artifact — NRMSE falls, and spread around that
  smaller mean falls faster.
- **Mean NRMSE gains per DIM step are shrinking**: 7→8 improved −31%,
  8→9 improved −15%, 9→10 improved −9.5%. The architecture is approaching
  an asymptote for NARMA under this baseline.

**Interpretation.** The CNN-init seed lottery dominates at small
reservoirs: at DIM 5 the best seed is ~24% better (relative NRMSE) than
the worst. By DIM 8 the top-to-bottom gap is only ~10%, and picking the
best seed over the median buys ~5%. The marginal value of running a CNN
seed survey decays sharply past DIM 7 — at DIM 8+ any reasonable seed
lands within noise of the best one, so the survey is mainly worth the
cost at DIM 5-6 where the lottery still matters.

This mirrors the classic law-of-large-numbers intuition: more neurons =
more independent features feeding the readout = less dependence on any
single init. It also suggests that the seed we freeze into
`DefaultSeed()` at DIM 8+ is largely cosmetic, while the DIM 5/6 choices
are load-bearing and should not be changed casually.
